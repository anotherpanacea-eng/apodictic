# APODICTIC Development Editor: Bibliography & Works Consulted

## Version 0.4.16
*Last Updated: February 21, 2026*

This bibliography collects theoretical sources, craft references, and fiction exemplars cited across the APODICTIC Development Editor framework. Sources were identified during three-model synthesis research (Claude/Opus, Codex, Gemini) and verified against audit and level-setting files.

**A note on citation accuracy:** Many of these references were surfaced by AI models during level-setting research. Publication details have been verified where possible, but some entries (particularly exact publication years for reprinted editions) may require confirmation. Where a source is paraphrased rather than directly quoted in the audit files, the citation reflects the concept attributed, not a direct quotation.

**Built with assistance from Claude (Anthropic), Codex (OpenAI), and Gemini (Google).**

## Fiction Exemplars

Authors and works cited as positive examples of specific craft techniques across APODICTIC audits. Organized by the primary craft dimension they exemplify.

### Literary Interiority & Consciousness

- Anderson, L. H. *Speak* (1999). Trauma-induced dissociation; visible onset, cost, and recovery.
- Cusk, R. *Outline* trilogy. Controlled surface; indirection generating interpretive pressure.
- Ferrante, E. *The Neapolitan Novels* (2011–2015). Ruthless psycho-narration; socially unacceptable thought.
- Larsen, N. *Passing* (1929). Performative suppression of interiority.
- Mantel, H. *Wolf Hall* (2009). Dense interiority for protagonist; historical consciousness.
- Milan, C. *The Duchess War* (2012). Autonomous female desire as plot engine.
- Morrison, T. *Beloved* (1987), *The Bluest Eye* (1970). Polyphonic consciousness under trauma.
- Tolstoy, L. *Anna Karenina* (1877). Unbroken female consciousness.
- Woolf, V. *Mrs. Dalloway* (1925), *To the Lighthouse* (1927). Stream-of-consciousness foundations.

### Literary Style & Form

- Berlin, L. *A Manual for Cleaning Women* (2015). Minimalist mode; relational asymmetry.
- Carson, A. Hybrid forms; reading experience impossible in conventional prose.
- Carver, R. Minimalist literary mode; restraint and omission.
- Johnson, D. *Jesus' Son* (1992). Compressed affect.
- Offill, J. *Dept. of Speculation* (2014). Fragmented form.
- Pynchon, T. Maximalist density; thematic discipline.
- Rankine, C. *Citizen* (2014). Hybrid experimental forms.
- Rushdie, S. Polyphonic modes; density management.
- Sebald, W. G. *The Rings of Saturn* (1995). Psychogeographic narrative; form as meaning.
- Wallace, D. F. Maximalist complexity; voice and motif discipline.

### Mystery, Thriller & Crime

- Auster, P. *City of Glass* (1985). Anti-mystery; deliberate non-solution.
- Chandler, R. *The Big Sleep* (1939), *The Long Goodbye* (1953). Atmosphere as information; noir ethics.
- Christie, A. *The Murder of Roger Ackroyd* (1926), *And Then There Were None* (1939). Fair-play puzzle; assumption exploitation.
- Connelly, M. (Harry Bosch series). Cumulative case logic; character through investigation.
- Eco, U. *The Name of the Rose* (1980). Full puzzle within literary-philosophical framework.
- Ellroy, J. Institutional complicity; noir power structures.
- Flynn, G. *Gone Girl* (2012). Epistemic warfare; domestic thriller; identity as puzzle domain.
- French, T. *In the Woods* (2007). Memory and trauma; investigative psychology.
- Hammett, D. *Red Harvest* (1929). Consequence-networking; noir realism.
- Highsmith, P. *The Talented Mr. Ripley* (1955), *Strangers on a Train* (1950). Inverted psychology; distance-based suspense.
- Iles, F. *Malice Aforethought* (1931). Inverted mystery; transparent culprit.
- Larsson, S. *The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo* (2005). Dual investigation; institutional threat.
- le Carré, J. *Tinker Tailor Soldier Spy* (1974). Information economy as institutional paranoia.
- Ludlum, R. *The Bourne Identity* (1980). Identity through combat; resource depletion.
- Sayers, D. L. *Gaudy Night* (1935). Community-based mystery; behavioral investigation.
- Ware, R. *In a Dark, Dark Wood* (2015). Domestic thriller; contained setting.

### Horror & Dark Fiction
