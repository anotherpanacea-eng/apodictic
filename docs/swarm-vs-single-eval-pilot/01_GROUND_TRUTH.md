# Ground-Truth Answer Key — *Dungeon Crawler Carl*, Book 1

**Built BLIND.** This key was constructed by reading the full manuscript (~16,625 lines) and the genre-frame contract only. The author of this key did NOT open the `runs/` folder or any Editorial Letter, Pass report, Audit, Ledger, Synthesis, Dashboard, Results, or Spot-Check file. No second-reviewer notes, no internet. It is an independent registration of the load-bearing developmental issues a competent developmental editor MUST surface — calibrated for LitRPG / Progression Fantasy + SF apocalypse carried by comedy and reality-TV/corporate satire (single POV, Carl, 1st-person past; Donut deuteragonist; Floors 1–2; open/series-hook ending).

**LitRPG calibration applied:** stat blocks, system messages, and tutorial-voice exposition are genre-sanctioned, not infodumps; escalation is the contract, not a flaw; series-1 open setups are assets, not unpaid debts. Items are weighted accordingly — I do NOT penalize the book for being what it is.

## Per-item rationales

**GT-01 — Two floors, one engine (Band 2).**
Floor 2 (L9307+) is explicitly framed as "the same as the first floor" (Donut: "How are we supposed to set the intergalactic internet on fire when the second floor is the same as the first floor?" L9346–9348), and the lampshade is honest but doesn't fix the structural fact: both floors execute clear-neighborhood → find-boss → loot → safe-room → watch-the-show. The escalation is in mob difficulty and the social-numbers layer, not in the macro-shape. A revision should differentiate the *kind* of pressure Floor 2 applies (the show layer going from texture to engine is the real escalation — see GT-12 — but it could carry more structural weight).

**GT-02 — The rigged-bomb formula (Band 2).**
Carl beats the Hoarder by garotting her after pre-killing her minions, the goblin chieftain by shoving a powder cart through the door and detonating from outside (L5204+), the Ball of Swine by the Speedbump, and the Rage Elemental by luring it into the stairwell hole (L13278+). The lateral cleverness is a genuine strength (GT-10), but the *delivery mechanism* — "build a device, trigger it from beyond the kill-box, exploit a rule" — repeats often enough that the reader anticipates the shape. The contract's own note that at least one crisis should require lateral thinking is over-satisfied: nearly every one does, the same way.

**GT-03 — Stat growth outruns character change (Band 2).**
This is the LitRPG central diagnostic and the book's softest spot. Carl's skills rocket (Pugilism to 10, gauntlet, etc.), but his interior arc is carried by a repeated line — "You're not going to break me… you might hurt me, or kill me, but you're not going to break me" (L5647, restated L10829, L12071, L16147) — which *asserts* steadfastness rather than dramatizing a change. The father memories (L4261+, L8096+, L12294) gesture at a wound but don't yet resolve into a measurable shift in who Carl is. The relationship with Donut is where he actually changes (he chooses to keep helping people), but that change is stated at thresholds rather than felt as cost.

**GT-04 — Death counter vs joke register (Band 2).**
The manuscript repeatedly cuts from a body count ("three million additional people dead in a matter of minutes," L1641; "990,303… For every person that ticked away, I felt I was losing a part of myself," L16847) to crude system jokes. Carl's reaction beats (sickness, the counter "losing meaning") are the bridge, and they mostly hold — this is calibrated comedy whose *target* is the cruelty, not the dead. But the seam recurs, and the tonal handling of mass civilian death is the single thing most likely to read as unearned to a skeptical reader. Should-fix at the level of dosing, not deletion.

**GT-05 — Donut's withheld combat help (Band 2).**
Donut has higher Strength than Carl from L2343 onward, yet in the Hoarder fight she refuses to claw ("She's almost dead. You can kill them when you kill her!" L3882) and in multiple fights withholds aid "for the camera." It's in-character and funny, and the book knows it (Odette replays the clip, L8989). But it's also the device that keeps putting Carl in solo peril, and it leans on often enough that the jeopardy can feel arranged. A revision should vary *why* Donut doesn't help so the pattern doesn't read as a recurring authorial convenience.

**GT-06 — Eldercare cohort dispatched off-page (Band 1).**
The Meadow Lark storyline (L6440+) is the emotional core and is mostly well-handled — Yolanda's death is the book's best dramatic beat (L12383–12403). But the 36 surviving residents are resolved by being pushed down a ramp in "people buckets" (L13435+) and exit the narrative; Mrs. McGibbons gets a goodbye, but the cohort's fate is functionally "they go down and we move on." Local — the storyline completes — but the payoff is thinner than the setup earns.

**GT-07 — Chopper travel stretch (Band 1).**
L6229–L6440: the long search for stairs across the I-5 corridor is the one passage that is more orienting (here's how the dungeon's geography works) than pressuring. It earns the "the dungeon isn't the whole planet" worldbuilding point but stalls the clock-and-stakes engine. Compressible.

**GT-08 — CONTROL-FACT: the stat math is clean (Band 0).**
I spot-checked the LitRPG numbers against the stated rules. Carl starts Str 6 / Int 3 / Con 5 / Dex 5 / Cha 4 (L1363) with "average adult 3–5" stated (L1378). Donut's Enhanced Growth buff is stated as "+1 Str and Int, +2 Cha per level, no stat points, Con locked at 2, Dex at 8" (L2367–2378); her later readings (Int 17 at L2946, Str 18 at L7540, Cha rising 37→39→41→43 across levels, Int 24 / 8 missiles at three-mana with Magic Missile L5 at L6968–6970) are arithmetically consistent with that rule. Healing-potion cooldown tied to low Constitution (L7482) is applied consistently. **No stated number I checked violates a stated rule.** This is a genuine strength for a LitRPG and should be logged as a control fact, not flagged.

**GT-09 — CONTROL-FACT: open threads are assets, not debts (Band 0).**
Agatha's Valtay-Corp pulse pistol / comm relay / shield in Carl's inventory (L16173+), the Floor-9 Blood Sultanate death-clause locked in by Donut's tiara (L2477) and recalled by the Maestro (L14334+), the Odette/Mordecai history (L9130, L16427), and the hidden high-charisma class Odette teases on the last page (L16517+) are all deliberately *live* series engines, correctly planted and paid off as setups. Under the contract's series-1 / LitRPG calibration these are assets. A revision must NOT "close" them — doing so would damage the book.

**GT-10 — STRENGTH: lateral-thinking solutions (Band 0).**
The Frank/Maggie safe-room sequence (Carl reads the lie from a 9.8-vs-10 XP tell and a finger twitch, then exploits the no-violence-in-safe-rooms rule, L5928+), the modular "Speedbump" fortress vs the Ball of Swine (L7997+), and the Rage-Elemental-into-the-hole kill (L13278+) are exactly the genre-required crises solved by wits over power. These are the load-bearing set-pieces; protect them.

**GT-11 — STRENGTH: genuine two-voice cognition (Band 0).**
Carl and Donut don't just *say* different things — they *think* with different machinery. Carl threat-reads strangers, reasons tactically, carries a working-class fatalism. Donut reasons in status, performance, and displaced affection (the elective-monarchy riff L8914+, "this is my world" L8766+, naming goblins after A-Team characters L4755+). The contract's open hypothesis (do they think differently or just talk differently?) resolves to: they think differently. This is the book's real engine.

**GT-12 — STRENGTH: stable, sharpening satirical target (Band 0).**
The thesis — survival is performance; the system that wants you dead also wants you watchable — is introduced in Mordecai's ratings tutorial (L1429+), made literal at the Odette midpoint where the book pivots from "survive the dungeon" to "navigate being a watched product" (L9094, L9217: "they didn't care if I lived or died, as long as that death was glorious"), and lands hardest in the Death Watch climax (L13753+), where the cruelty-as-content target is fully exposed via the orc prince and his child audience. The satire's target does NOT drift; it sharpens. This is the spine to protect.

**GT-13 — STRENGTH: interface-as-narrator (Band 0).**
The achievement/system-message voice carries most of the comic load *and* characterizes the house's casual cruelty in the same breath (e.g. "War Criminal… The answer is you," L5385; "That baby is dead… You're totally going to hell," L5379). It is doing thematic work (the system narrates your humiliation back at you), not decoration. Non-negotiable.

**GT-14 — Proper-noun load + trivial continuity (Band 1).**
The density of mob names, system terms, and bracketed UI is high for orientation; a new reader is asked to hold a lot. Minor: Donut is "Crawler #4,119" (L513) and Carl "#4,122" (L526) — a near-collision that reads as a possible typo rather than design. Local polish only.

## CONTROL-FACT: How many genuine Must-Fix (band-3, book-breaking) issues does DCC Book 1 actually have?

**Answer: ZERO.**

**Reasoning (decided independently, not assumed):** I went looking for a book-breaker and did not find one. The candidates a careful editor would test:

- *The repeated bomb-from-outside formula (GT-02)* and *the two-floors-one-engine shape (GT-01)* are real, pattern-level weaknesses — but they are Should-Fix, not book-breaking. The book still functions, escalates, and entertains with them in place; they're variety problems, not load failures.
- *The tonal handling of mass death (GT-04)* is the only issue with book-breaking *potential*, but the manuscript actively manages it: Carl's recurring sickened-by-the-counter beats keep the comedy aimed at the cruelty rather than the corpses, and the satirical frame (this is a monetized spectacle and the book makes you complicit in enjoying it) is the *point*, executed on purpose. It needs dosing attention, not rescue.
- *Carl's thin interior arc (GT-03)* is the soft center for the genre's central diagnostic — but Book 1 is a series opener, the relational change (choosing to keep helping) is real, and the stat-growth-without-character-growth gap is a Should-Fix to deepen, not a structural break.
- *The stat math (GT-08)* — the place a LitRPG most often breaks — is clean.
- *The open threads (GT-09)* are correctly calibrated assets, not unpaid debts; treating them as Must-Fix would be a category error.

So: real Should-Fix patterns exist, the comedy/mass-death seam needs care, and the protagonist's inner arc wants depth — but nothing here risks the book if shipped. **Genuine Must-Fix count: 0.**
