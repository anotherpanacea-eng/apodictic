## Full text (as pasted)

Legal-Risk Detection Playbook for Manuscript-Editing Tools — five risk classes (Defamation; Privacy/Disclosure; Rights-Clearance: Copyright/Trademark/Contracts; Right of Publicity / Misappropriation; Ancillary Publishing Torts — IIED, Breach of Confidence, Incitement), each with the 7 fields (detectable textual signals; clarifying questions; escalation cues; route-to-counsel threshold; false-positive traps; jurisdiction caveats; genre nuance), plus a consolidated Escalation-Trigger Taxonomy matrix, a "Confidence & Gaps" section, and a fully URL-cited "Sources" / "Works cited" list.

**Key cases + statutes cited (jurisdiction):**
- *New York Times Co. v. Sullivan*, 376 U.S. 254 (1964) — US, actual malice.
- *Time, Inc. v. Hill*, 385 U.S. 374 (1967) + *Cantrell v. Forest City*, 419 U.S. 245 (1974) — US, false light / actual malice.
- *Bindrim v. Mitchell*, 92 Cal. App. 3d 61 (1979) — US, libel in fiction (identifiability via unique therapeutic methodology).
- *Bonome v. Kaysen*, 2004 WL 1194731 (Mass. Super. 2004) — US, memoir disclosure of intimate sexual/medical facts.
- *Haynes v. Alfred A. Knopf*, 8 F.3d 1222 (7th Cir. 1993) — US, public-record + newsworthiness defeats private-facts claim.
- *Renwick v. News & Observer* — US (NC), rejects false-light tort.
- *Hustler Magazine v. Falwell*, 485 U.S. 46 (1988) — US, IIED needs actual malice for public figures.
- *Salinger v. Random House*, 811 F.2d 90 (2d Cir. 1987) — US, unpublished works weigh against fair use.
- *Suntrust Bank v. Houghton Mifflin*, 268 F.3d 1257 (11th Cir. 2001) — US, transformative parody fair use (*The Wind Done Gone*).
- *Rogers v. Grimaldi*, 875 F.2d 994 (2d Cir. 1989) — US, artistic-relevance test ("wholly unrelated" / "disguised advertisement").
- *Olivia de Havilland v. FX Networks*, 21 Cal. App. 5th 845 (2018) — US, First Amendment protects portrayal of real people in expressive works; rejects mandatory clearance.
- *Rice v. Paladin Enterprises*, 128 F.3d 233 (4th Cir. 1997) — US, publisher liability for an explicit murder manual (aiding/abetting; outside First Amendment).
- New York Civil Rights Law § 50-f — US (NY), 40-yr post-mortem right of publicity + digital-replica/deepfake provisions; CA = 70 yrs.
- UK Defamation Act 2013 s.1 + *Lachaux v Independent Print* [2019] UKSC 27 — serious-harm threshold requires proof of actual/probable harm.
- *Campbell v MGN Ltd* [2004] UKHL 22 — UK, misuse of private information (Arts 8/10 ECHR).
- *Coco v. A.N. Clark (Engineers) Ltd* [1968] FSR 415 — UK, three-part breach-of-confidence test; *Spycatcher* prior-publication defense; *Wilkinson v Downton* / Protection from Harassment Act 1997 — UK IIED analogues.
- Australia Defamation Act 2005 + Model Defamation Amendment Provisions 2020 — serious-harm element.
