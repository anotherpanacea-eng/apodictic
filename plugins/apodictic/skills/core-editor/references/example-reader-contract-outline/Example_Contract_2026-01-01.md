# Contract and Controlling Idea — Example

Canonical worked-example Contract for the reader-contract reverse outline. The schema block below is
projected verbatim into the outline's §The Reader Contract (two-sided, R2); the clause denominator is
recomputed from it (R7). An empty field (`HEAT LEVEL:`) is legitimate and renders as its literal empty
state (`HEAT LEVEL: —`) — it is never dropped.

## Contract Schema

GENRE/SUBGENRE: literary fiction
READER PROMISE: a woman escapes a dying town; the town's buried secret is exposed
CONTROLLING IDEA: leaving costs more than staying, but staying costs the truth
HEAT LEVEL: 
DARKNESS LEVEL: moderate
PRIMARY TENSION TYPE: moral
ENDING TYPE: open
TONE COMPS: quiet, elegiac
STRUCTURE COMPS: linear
NON-NEGOTIABLES: the lighthouse stays unlit; Mara does not return to the valley

## Contract Statement

A woman claws her way out of a valley the world has forgotten, and in doing so uncovers the lie the
town told itself to keep going — but escape and exposure pull against each other, and she cannot have
both cleanly.

---

## Controlling Idea

**Format:** [Value] + [Cause]

**Statement:** Leaving costs more than staying, but staying costs the truth — because the town's
survival depends on a shared silence that only a departure can break.

## Anti-Idea

**What this book is explicitly NOT arguing:** that small towns are simply prisons to flee, or that
exposure is always the moral choice.
