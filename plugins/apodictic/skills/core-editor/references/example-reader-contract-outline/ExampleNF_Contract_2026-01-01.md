# Contract and Controlling Idea — Narrative-Nonfiction Example

Canonical narrative-nonfiction Contract for the reader-contract reverse outline. It appends the
optional narrative-nonfiction block (`CENTRAL QUESTION` / `PROMISE TYPE`, prereq B / PR #177). The
CENTRAL QUESTION drives the deliverable's single idea clause in place of CONTROLLING IDEA (§4a).

## Contract Schema

GENRE/SUBGENRE: narrative nonfiction / investigative
READER PROMISE: the reader learns how the dam failed and who chose to bury the warning
CONTROLLING IDEA: 
HEAT LEVEL: 
DARKNESS LEVEL: 
PRIMARY TENSION TYPE: epistemic
ENDING TYPE: closed
TONE COMPS: measured, investigative
STRUCTURE COMPS: braided timeline
NON-NEGOTIABLES: the engineer's warning is quoted verbatim
CENTRAL QUESTION: why did the dam fail when the failure had been predicted
PROMISE TYPE: Reveal

## Contract Statement

A public catastrophe turns out to have been foretold in a filed-and-forgotten memo; the book traces
how a documented warning became an evacuated town, and names the choice that connected them.
