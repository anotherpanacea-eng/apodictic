# Pass 0 — Reverse Outline (narrative-nonfiction worked example)

Objective reverse outline of a narrative-nonfiction manuscript, exercising the reader-contract outline
against a Contract that carries the optional CENTRAL QUESTION / PROMISE TYPE block (prereq B). The
idea clause of the deliverable is driven by CENTRAL QUESTION here, not CONTROLLING IDEA.

### S1
What happens: the reservoir dam cracks during the spring melt and the town below is evacuated.
What the reader now knows: the failure was sudden, public, and nearly fatal.

### S2
What happens: a reporter finds a decade-old engineer's memo warning of the exact failure mode.
What the reader now knows: someone predicted this in writing, and the warning was filed and forgotten.

### S3
What happens: the county board minutes show the memo was tabled to protect a bond rating.
What the reader now knows: the silence was a choice with a price tag, not an oversight.
