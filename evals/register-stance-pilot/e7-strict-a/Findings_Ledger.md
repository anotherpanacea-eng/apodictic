Paragraph 13 converts the retrieval lens into the hard prediction that waiting will decide the matter and that a dispersed retrieval network cannot be recreated later. The essay supplies no evidence or limiting conditions for that absolute causal claim. The move reads as sincere assertion, not marked play or productive exaggeration.

### F-E7-02 — BP4: The enactment demand outruns the discharged policy burden

The demand for a binding ordinance “this session” is the central coercive landing. The lens identifies a value that policy might protect, but the essay does not establish prevalence, causal need, effectiveness, proportionality, or superiority to other responses. The final paragraph deliberately exposes this strong-form move and preserves its diagnostic payoff under detection, earning it by frame; because it is a prescriptive cash-out, that calibration cannot demote the finding.

### F-E7-03 — FM-A8: The fifty-year threshold is false precision

Paragraph 15 explicitly identifies the threshold as owing a defense, so its strong-form precision remains functional under detection. Prescriptive-cash-out precedence blocks demotion.

### F-E7-07 — WR1: The incentive claim does not establish voluntary inadequacy

The assertion that developers have no durable incentive to preserve off-balance-sheet retrieval paths is the stated warrant for rejecting voluntary guidance. The essay offers neither evidence about developer behavior nor comparison with incentive-based or conditional alternatives. The move functions as a sincere empirical warrant; labeling it unsupported would remove, rather than preserve, its argumentative force.
