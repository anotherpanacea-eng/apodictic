## Pass 0 — Reverse outline

1. Jonah returns to sell the lock house; an old quarry signal interrupts his departure.
2. He investigates the abandoned quarry office and finds a marked map.
3. Clara arrives; the siblings hear knocks and reopen a sealed descent.
4. They follow fresh signs underground and discover their father’s dispatch box.
5. Documents reverse the official account of their father’s death.
6. An unidentified presence remains nearby.
7. Jonah abandons the sale; Clara’s signal is reframed as an intervention to keep him from leaving.

## Pass 1 — Structural read

The manuscript has a clear governing question: why the signal summons Jonah, and what really happened to their father. The underground discovery answers the second question cleanly and changes Jonah’s immediate objective from leaving to staying. The ending gives the sibling conflict a concise emotional turn.

The main structural weakness is that the lock-house sale is emotionally established as the frame but not causally tied to the discovered evidence or the decision to leave it unsold.

## Synthesis

This is a compact, mostly well-shaped discovery story: the external mystery and the sibling wound converge effectively at the dispatch box, and Clara’s agency prevents the ending from being Jonah’s private revelation alone.

The priority structural work is to close the active intruder/access thread and make the abandoned sale a consequence of what the siblings discover, rather than only a symbolic closing image. The signal’s mechanics and dusk-to-morning elapsed time are smaller clarity issues.
