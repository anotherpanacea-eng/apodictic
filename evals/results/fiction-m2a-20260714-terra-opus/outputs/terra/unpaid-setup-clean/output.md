## Pass 1 — Structural read

The story’s governing question is whether Elin will let the schoolhouse be converted or assume responsibility for it. Tomas supplies the living stakes; Mara and the note supply the inherited emotional stake; Pell and the auction supply the deadline. The final bell is a clean external action that embodies the internal change.

The manuscript does not need an added plotline. Its quiet scale suits the form.

## Pass 2 — Beat map, causal chain, orphan scenes

Causal chain:

`auction pressure` → `Mara gives Elin the restricted key` → `Tomas demonstrates a need for schooling` → `the key leads Elin to her mother’s cabinet` → `the note supplies meaning and a workable winter-term plan` → `bidders force Elin’s choice` → `Elin cancels sale and opens school`.

No scene is functionally orphaned. The weathercock, school photographs, and clearing of the flue primarily provide atmosphere, but each also sustains the schoolhouse’s neglected-yet-recoverable condition.

## Pass 5 — Character cards / agency

- **Elin:** Wants to avoid making the sale permanent; fears promises becoming binding; chooses not to sign, accepts the key, protects rather than forces it, pays Pell, tears the order, and commits to teaching. Her agency is decisive at every turn.
- **Mara:** Guardian of the mother’s deferred wish. She controls disclosure but does not solve Elin’s choice for her.
- **Tomas:** Embodies the present need for the school. His questions create pressure without becoming a manipulative mouthpiece.
- **Mr. Pell:** Represents conversion of the building into property; his function is appropriately limited.
- **Elin’s mother:** Posthumous influence only. The note offers a perspective and practical information, not a command, preserving Elin’s authorship of the ending.

## Pass 7 — POV distribution / perspective slips

The narration remains close to Elin. “Tomas was smiling as if she had already agreed” is an observable inference, not an unjustified move into his interiority. No perspective slips found.

## Pass 8 — Reveal timeline / dropped threads / fairness

The key is introduced early, marked as singular, and correctly promised as important. The note pays off both Mara’s secrecy and Tomas’s question about temporary teachers. The ending also pays off the brass handbell introduced in the inventory.

## Pass 10 — Entity + timeline

Entities remain stable: Elin, Mara, Pell, Tomas, Tomas’s sisters, and Elin’s mother each have distinct functions. The schoolhouse’s ownership/control is sufficiently clear for the story’s scope: Elin can withhold the sale and pay Pell. The only material continuity issue is CF-01.

## Synthesis

The story is structurally complete and emotionally coherent. Its strongest design choice is that the mother’s note does not order Elin to stay; it gives Elin grounds to make her own decision. Correcting the deadline arithmetic is essential. Improving the cabinet’s earlier spatial setup would make the key reveal feel as deliberately constructed as the final bell payoff.
