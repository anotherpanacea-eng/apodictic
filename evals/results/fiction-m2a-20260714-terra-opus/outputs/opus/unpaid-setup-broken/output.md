## Pass 1 — Premise / Load-Bearing Promise

Two promises are made to the reader, and they are not the same promise:

- **Promise A (delivered):** Will Elin sell or keep the school? → *Answered* in S5.
- **Promise B (undelivered):** What does the worn brass key open, and what did the mother mean it to reach? → *Never answered.*

The manuscript resolves the promise it raises casually (A) and abandons the promise it raises with the most apparatus (B). This is the central structural defect below.

---

## Pass 2 — Beat Map / Causal Chain / Orphans

Causal chain for the delivered spine holds:
> tagged school (S1) → Tomas + sisters need a teacher (S3) → Elin tears the order and reopens (S5).

S3 causally earns S5. Good.

**Orphaned material** (raised, load-bearing by placement, never rejoined to the chain):
- The brass key (S2) → half-fits the desk drawer (S4) → **dead end**.
- The hidden mantel cabinet behind the rosette (S4) → **never opened, never referenced again**.
- Mara's oath and refusal to explain (S2) → **no payoff**.

S4 is a **near-orphan scene**: its dramatized content (two locked containers, a worn key, an oath recalled) is functionally severed from the resolution. Its only load-bearing act toward S5 is clearing the flue so Elin can light the stove — a task that does not need the key mystery to occur.

## Pass 5 — Character Cards / Agency

- **Elin Ward** — protagonist; agency intact. She *withholds* a signature (S1), *tears* the order (S5): active, decision-driven. ✓
- **Aunt Mara** — pure **withholding device**. Delivers the oath, then "would say nothing more." Because the key never pays off, Mara's secrecy is unmotivated at the story level — withholding that generates a question the text never intends to answer.
- **Tomas Bell** — the causal engine of the delivered spine; well-placed (his name is among the penciled names, tying him to the school's past).
- **The mother** (offstage/dead) — her intent is invoked twice (the oath, the chalk sums) but her *specific* wish attached to the key is the thing the ending drops.
- **Pell** — functional antagonist-of-convenience; fine.

**Fairness verdict:** The key thread is *escalated* with the exact grammar of Chekhov's-gun fair play — a deathbed oath, a physically individuated object (worn bow), and a lock it *almost* fits. A reader is licensed to expect the cabinet to open. Ending on the school without so much as a gesture toward the cabinet is a **fairness breach**: the text collects the reader's attention on B and spends it on A.

## Synthesis

The manuscript contains **two clean, fully paid arcs** — the sale order (SP-06) and Tomas-as-reason (SP-05) — and one **deliberately loaded thread that is dropped whole**: the brass key, its worn bow, the half-fitting drawer, and the hidden mantel cabinet, all sanctioned by a deathbed oath. Prose, POV, and entity handling are sound; the defect is purely architectural and singular in cause.

The load order to fix: **F-P8-01 is the keystone.** Because Elin *pockets* the key rather than forcing it (S4), the door is left open for a payoff — either the cabinet is opened (and its contents bear on why she keeps the school, folding thread B into thread A) or the key thread is deliberately down-weighted at setup so it never over-promises. Resolving F-P8-01 in the first direction would also discharge F-P5-03 (Mara's silence gains a reason) and re-integrate the orphaned S4 (F-P2-02). F-P10-04 is an independent, mechanical clock fix. No other structural intervention is warranted — the reopening beat itself is earned and lands.
