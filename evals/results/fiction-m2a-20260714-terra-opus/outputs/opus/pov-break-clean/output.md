## Pass 0 — Reverse Outline (as-written)
1. Dusk; Jonah sees the signal lamp "wink twice" above the flooded quarry; decides it is for him.
2. Backstory: he came to *sell* the lock house; sale completes "by noon tomorrow."
3. Office is shuttered "since the pumps failed"; no boat, no figure — yet the lamp shines.
4. He climbs the cinder road to the office.
5. Door ajar; he calls Clara, then recalls she vowed never to enter.
6. Interior: dust, "old shift ledgers … on a shelf," the lamp "warm beside an oil can."
7. Chimney warm — lit within the hour; beneath it a survey map, eastern shaft circled in blue.
8. Backstory: father died in that shaft per the company, after a three-day search.
9. Clara appears in the doorway.
10. "You saw it too." — "I saw you leave the path. The lamp was already dark."
11. Wick smokes; smell of extinguished oil; Jonah can't read her.
12. He shows the map; Clara looks to "the shelf where their father's ledgers should have been."
13. "We should go. The ground isn't safe."
14. Three measured knocks from under the floor.
15. Both hear them; knocks repeat.
16. They pry the swollen trapdoor.
17. Beat of sibling history (father favored Jonah for errands).
18. Trap opens on a ladder; he recalls the company sealing this passage.
19. "Stay here." — "That worked so well last time."
20. Descent; drainage gallery toward the eastern shaft.
21. Fresh boot marks; he measures a heel; hears Clara start down.
22. Clara follows.
23. Timber door: company seal *cut*, a new chain looped but unlocked.
24. Dry chamber, camp stool, tin dispatch box from father's desk.
25. Pay sheets; beneath, letters documenting wall cracks "weeks before the collapse."
26. "He knew."
27. Reveal: father reported the danger; company continued the shift, then blamed him.
28. A scrape; Jonah shutters the lamp; the boot-mark maker "did not approach."
29. Aside: his mailed letter to the buyer hasn't arrived; he hopes the sale can be stopped.
30. Back up; rain thins to morning.
31. Clara sets the lamp in the window; Jonah "understood then that she had lit it."
32. "You might have told me." — "You might have stayed long enough to listen."
33. He lays the key by the lamp; they descend to phone the inspector; house unsold, signal burning.

**Driving question (Pass 1):** *Who lit the signal, and what does it want Jonah to see?* The manuscript answers half of this (Clara lit it; the dispatch box is what she wanted him to see) but silently abandons the other half (the underground presence).

## Pass 2 — Beat Map / Causal Chain / Orphan Scenes

The causal spine is sound through beat 27. The rupture is the **underground agent**. Three physical facts assert a living third party below: the *three measured knocks* (14–15, which **cause** the descent), the *fresh boot marks* whose heel Jonah bothers to measure (21), the *cut seal and freshly-looped chain* (23), and the *scrape* of something that "did not approach" (28). These are all pointing fingers — and the story ends without a hand attached to any of them. Clara cannot be the source: she is upstairs with Jonah when the knocks sound. The father is dead. The chamber was *staged* by someone, and the knocks were *made* by someone, and the manuscript closes the file on both.

apodictic.finding.v1
- id: F-P2-01
- pass: Pass 2 (causal chain / orphan)
- severity: **Must-Fix**
- evidence_refs: "three measured knocks from somewhere under the floor"; "Fresh boot marks stippled the mud … measuring the heel against his palm"; "Someone had cut the company's seal and looped a new chain"; "whoever had made the boot marks did not approach"
- mechanism: The knocks are the proximate cause of the trapdoor being opened and thus of the discovery — they are load-bearing, not atmosphere. They, the boot marks, and the freshly cut seal all posit a present, deliberate human agent in the gallery. The ending reassigns *the lamp* to Clara but leaves this agent wholly unaccounted for. A reader who tracks cause to effect is left holding an unresolved live actor: the story's central mystery ("who signaled Jonah, and who is down there?") is answered only in part while presenting as fully closed. Either the agent must be identified/dismissed (e.g., a company man returning to remove the evidence, the buyer's surveyor, a third sibling), or the physical traces must be rewritten so no living underground presence is implied.

Her agency is the story's best mechanism — *but* it is contaminated by F-P2-01: because an unexplained third party also acts on the same night, the reader cannot cleanly credit Clara with the staging, which dilutes her reveal.
- **The father (foreman)** — offstage cause; card coherent (reported danger, scapegoated).
- **The underground agent** — an actor with three tracked actions and zero identity. Effectively an uncharacterized character. See F-P2-01.

apodictic.finding.v1
- id: F-P8-01
- pass: Pass 8 (orphaned setup / motivation)
- severity: Should-Fix
- evidence_refs: "The letter Jonah had mailed before leaving the city had not yet reached the buyer; he could only hope the sale could still be stopped."
- mechanism: This is the sole appearance of a mailed letter, introduced at beat 29 with no prior plant, and it contradicts the established premise. Jonah "had come home to sell the lock house" (beat 2), yet he had already, *before leaving the city*, mailed the buyer a letter to *stop* the sale — reversing his stated purpose before the inciting signal ever appears. The thread is both unplanted and logically at odds with his arc's starting position (the point of the ending is that the discovery *changes* his mind about selling; a pre-existing letter to halt the sale pre-empts that change). Cut it, or plant his ambivalence earlier so the letter reads as a prior wavering rather than a contradiction.

## Synthesis

The manuscript is technically clean where craft usually fails — POV is airtight, the two real reveals (Clara's lamp, the father's exoneration) are both fairly planted and paid, and the compression is disciplined. The structural weight sits on **one Must-Fix**: the story stages an underground human presence — knocks, measured boot marks, a freshly cut seal, a scrape — and then resolves only the *lamp*, silently abandoning the actor those traces demand (**F-P2-01 / SP-02 / SP-03**). Because that agent is left live, it also bleeds credit away from Clara's otherwise excellent reveal. Second priority is the contradictory mailed-letter thread (**F-P8-01**), which reverses the protagonist's premise before the plot begins. The ledger (**CF-01**) and lamp-signaling (**CF-02**) snags are smaller reconciliations. Fix the underground agent and the story closes cleanly; leave it and the ending only *feels* resolved.

Priority order: F-P2-01 → F-P8-01 → CF-01 → CF-02 → CF-03 / F-P2-02.
