## Prefatory Note: Satire Identification Protocol

Before any step runs, the audit must register a structural feature that governs all subsequent diagnosis: this text operates at two argumentative levels simultaneously.

**Surface argument (persona's argument):** A rational economic proposer argues that Irish infants should be sold as food to relieve poverty, reduce Papist population, benefit landlords, and stimulate trade. This argument is internally coherent, arithmetically supported, and structurally complete on its own terms.

**Embedded argument (Swift's actual argument):** By performing the persona's argument with merciless precision, Swift argues that British colonial policy toward Ireland, combined with Irish landlord rapacity and political indifference, has reduced human beings to livestock — and that the political class that refuses real remedies is morally equivalent to cannibals. The real claim is normative and evaluative, delivered through sustained ironic performance rather than direct statement.

The audit must diagnose BOTH levels where relevant and invoke Step 9's Distinguish Protocol to classify correctly. Running the audit on the persona's argument alone — as if Swift sincerely proposed eating children — would produce a false-positive "sound" verdict on a monstrous argument. Running it on the surface text as if it were straightforwardly defective would produce false-negative "unsound" on a masterwork of political argument. The correct procedure: diagnose the persona's argument for what it claims to be, then apply Distinguish to recover Swift's argument and classify accordingly.

This note is logged before diagnosis begins and will anchor Step 9.

## STEP 2: Claim Ladder & Definition Stability (CL)

**Run on:** full essay.

### Persona-Level Claim Ladder

```
C0 (main claim): The children of Irish poor should be sold as food
  at one year old, yielding economic benefit to parents, landlords,
  the nation, and the trade.

C1: The current situation is a genuine crisis — 120,000 children
  annually cannot be supported by existing means.
C2: The children can be profitably consumed: nutritious, salable,
  and seasonally available.
C3: The proposal generates cascading economic benefits: national
  income, landlord profit, mother's incentive, reduced Papist
  population, stimulus to marriage and care.

STAKES: Moral and consequential — without this remedy, 120,000
  children are a charge on the state and their parents; the crisis
  is irresolvable by any other method.
  Stakes type: CONSEQUENTIAL + PRACTICAL

KEY TERMS:
  T1: "children" = human infants, aged ~one year; operationally
      treated as livestock throughout
  T2: "beneficial to the publick" = generates economic return;
      the civic/moral register of "benefit" is evacuated
  T3: "projectors" = social reformers proposing schemes; used
      dismissively to discredit conventional approaches
  T4: "expedients" = genuine policy alternatives, presented by the
      persona as idle and visionary
```

### CL Diagnosis at Persona Level

The persona's claim ladder is formally coherent. C1, C2, and C3 are necessary and non-circular. T1 ("children") undergoes a register shift — the opening treats children as human sufferers deserving rescue; after the proposal drops, they are consistently treated as livestock. This is definitional smuggling.

**Code: CL4** — "children" shifts from the moral/human register to the commodity register mid-essay and the shift does the proposal's central argumentative work. The shift is, of course, intentional on Swift's part — it is the satirical engine — but at the persona-argument level it is a genuine definitional slide.

### Swift's Actual Claim Ladder

```
C0 (Swift's claim): England's colonial exploitation of Ireland and
  the Irish landlord class's indifference have reduced the Irish
  poor to a condition indistinguishable from livestock; the only
  "rational" solution available within that value system is
  cannibalism, and any government that refuses the real remedies
  is morally responsible for this degradation.

C1: The poverty of the Irish poor is real, severe, and well-
  documented by observation.
C2: The political and economic class has systematically refused
  workable remedies (taxation of absentees, domestic manufacture,
  landlord mercy).
C3: Within the utilitarian-economic framework that class uses to
  govern Ireland, the proposal is internally consistent — therefore
  the framework itself is monstrous.

STAKES: Moral — a wrong is being done; the political class is
  complicit; the essay forces complicit readers to feel the
  weight of their own indifference.
  Stakes type: MORAL + EPISTEMIC

KEY TERMS (Swift's level):
  T1: "beneficial to the publick" — Swift exposes the term as
      evacuated of genuine civic meaning; the satire turns on
      the contrast between the phrase's normal register and its
      operational content here.
  T2: "expedients" — the real remedies; Swift presents them
      through the persona's dismissal, which makes their rejection
      visible as a political choice rather than a practical necessity.
```

**Code at Swift's level: PASS** — the claim ladder is recoverable, stable, and powerful. No CL failures at the actual-argument level.

## STEP 6: Objection Handling & Dialectical Integrity (OB, DI)

**Run on:** full essay — concessive moves, anticipated counterarguments, structural gaps.

### 6a. Strongest Objection (text-internal)

The **strongest text-internal objection** to the persona's argument is not the objection about reducing the population (which the persona addresses directly and cheerfully): "I freely own, and it was indeed one principal design." Nor is it the moral objection about cruelty (which is waved away as "unjustly" raised).

The load-bearing text-internal objection is: **the premise that no other expedients are workable is asserted without demonstration.** The entire proposal rests on the claim that genuine alternatives — taxation of absentees, domestic manufacture, landlord mercy — are "vain, idle, visionary." The persona asserts this claim but the assertion is the argument's weight-bearing strut. If the alternatives are workable, the proposal collapses. The persona's response — "let no man talk to me of these expedients till he hath some glimpse of hope that there will ever be a hearty and sincere attempt to put them into practice" — is a non-answer: it shifts the burden to opponents without providing the proof.

**At Swift's level**, the strongest objection inverts: the real objection is that Swift has not demonstrated the listed remedies would actually work if enacted. The satirical performance proves the political will is absent; it does not prove the remedies are adequate. Swift's argument would be stronger if the genuine remedies had been argued for directly elsewhere (they were, in other Swift writings — but not within this essay).

```
STRONGEST OBJECTION: The claim that no alternatives are workable
  is the proposal's load-bearing premise and is asserted, not
  demonstrated. The enumeration of alternatives ("taxing our
  absentees," "landlords to have at least one degree of mercy")
  and their dismissal as "vain, idle, visionary" performs the
  defeat of alternatives without arguing it.
  
  Engaged: Y (the persona addresses this — but evasively)
  Quality: EVASION — the text acknowledges the alternatives exist,
    names them, then dismisses them by asserting hopelessness
    rather than demonstrating inadequacy. The dismissal is
    procedurally dishonest: it substitutes despair for argument.
  Dialectical integrity: PSEUDO-RESOLUTION — the persona talks as
    if the alternatives have been disposed of when they have only
    been asserted over
  Code: OB4 (concession without cost — the alternatives are named
    but the naming changes nothing); DI2 (pseudo-resolution —
    dismissal treated as refutation)
```

### Objection Inventory

```

  No genuine alternatives exist.
  Engaged: Y — EVASION. Named but dismissed by assertion.
  Code: OB4, DI2

OBJECTION 2:
  The proposal is cruel — it involves killing and eating children.
  Engaged: Y — STRAWMAN/EVASION hybrid.
  The persona concedes "some scrupulous people might be apt to

  then dismisses this as "indeed very unjustly" raised. The
  objection is not engaged; it is named and waved away. The moral
  objection receives no counter-argument at all.
  Quality: EVASION
  Dialectical integrity: DI0 (starting-point smuggling — the

  Code: OB2, DI0

OBJECTION 3:
  The population reduction will harm the kingdom's long-term

  Engaged: Y — SUBSTANTIVE (partial).
  The persona addresses this directly: "I freely own [this was]

  Quality: COSMETIC — the objection is addressed but the answer
```

## STEP 8: Cross-Section Integrity

**Run on:** full essay, comparing sections against each other.

### 8a. Claim Drift

| Section | Claim as Stated | Qualification Level | Drift from C0 |
|---------|----------------|--------------------|----|
| Opening | "find out a fair, cheap and easy method" for children | Hedged: "I have always found them grossly mistaken" | None |
| Proposal | "humbly offer it to publick consideration" — infants as food | Confident: "I make no doubt" | None (proposal drops) |

| Closing | "I profess in the sincerity of my heart" | Confident + sincere | Stable — returns to opening register of public concern |

**Claim drift: STABLE** at the persona level. One significant definitional slide noted: "the publick" in the opening means "the nation including the poor"; "the publick" in the closing means "those persons of quality and fortune" who benefit from the scheme. This is CL4 (definition drift on "publick good") and is structurally intentional — it demonstrates that the persona's concept of public benefit has invisibly narrowed to exclude the very people it claimed to serve.

**Code: CL4** — "publick" / "publick good" shifts from inclusive civic meaning to exclusive class benefit. This is a Swift-level deliberate device reinforcing the satire: the persona's economics always served the rich, despite the charitable framing.

### 8b. Qualification Erosion

Opening sections hedge: "I have always found them grossly mistaken." The proposal section hedges formally: "I humbly offer." The advantages section abandons hedging entirely: "I can think of no one objection." The closing re-performs sincerity but not qualification.

**Code: WR3 (dynamic)** — qualification language is present early but absent by the enumeration of advantages. The persona's confidence grows with the argument's internal elaboration rather than with additional external evidence. This is FM-A16 (Qualification Erosion).

### 8c. Scope Accumulation

The proposal is explicitly scope-limited in the alternatives section ("I calculate my remedy for this one individual Kingdom of Ireland, and for no other"). But the advantages — reducing Papists, stimulating marriage, benefiting landlords, increasing trade — progressively assert effects on the kingdom's spiritual composition, family structure, class relations, and international trade. The local scope claim arrives late, after the benefits have been asserted universally within Ireland.

**Code: BP2 (local)** — not fully scope-creeping to the universal, but the benefits are asserted more broadly than the explicit scope restriction in the final enumeration would allow.

### 8d. Definition Stability
