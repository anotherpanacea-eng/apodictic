**Reasoning.** The piece is first-person witness ("I learned this," "In month five I reorganized the bookshelf"). The dominant function is testimonial: a bereaved narrator reports the experience of grief outliving the social support that surrounds it. But it does not stop at observation — it advances an interpretive/evaluative claim about a shared cultural practice ("There is a casserole logic to the way we are taught to mourn... we have agreed... to pretend they are the same clock"). That move is AT2/AT4-interpretive, not pure AT4-observational. Per the spec's AT4 burden split, I diagnose **within** AT4 rather than reclassifying.

This is NOT AT3. There is no proposal, no "we should," no recommendation, no normative directive. Flagging any of them here would be a false positive.

**Audience calibration.** Per the genre rubric ("Personal Essay (argument-shaped): AT4 or AT2 typically... apply CL-codes loosely... use the Distinguish Protocol aggressively to avoid false positives"). The essay does not assume specialist warrants. **No AC-code fires.** Advisory only: the essay leans on a sympathetic reader who will grant the universal "we," but that is consonant with the form, not an AC3 flattery-drift failure, because the essay still does the structural work of *showing* (not merely asserting) the two-clocks dynamic through dated, concrete scenes.

## Step 6: Objection Handling & Dialectical Integrity (OB, DI)

**Run on:** full draft.

### 6a. The single strongest objection (named first)

**STRONGEST OBJECTION (text-internal):** *"Your evidence is your own grief plus one instance of you bringing a lasagna — how do you get from that to a claim about what 'we' have collectively 'agreed' to pretend? The empty refrigerator may mean the support system is rationed, not that grief has a different clock; and your own count of one casserole-recipient (the friend you brought lasagna to) is the only outside data point, and you admit you misread HER silence. So either you misread her, or you're now over-reading your own case. The 'two clocks' may be one clock with a long tail you happen to be living in."*

This objection is **text-internal**: it turns the essay's own central evidence (the reciprocity anecdote — the narrator's confessed misreading of her friend's silence) and its own central warrant (a personal case licenses a claim about "we") against the C0 generalization. It is sharper than the canonical imported objection ("personal essays can't prove general claims"), which applies equally to every essay in the genre.

- **Engaged: PARTIALLY / IMPLICITLY.** The essay does not *name* this objection, but it pre-empts a large share of it structurally: by confessing the narrator's own failure as a comforter ("I told myself the quiet meant she was healing"), it builds the two-sided account that answers "maybe it's just you." It does NOT close the residual gap — that the very anecdote proving the "we" is also an admission that the narrator, too, may have misread a griever — which could equally support a *different* reading (we simply can't know each other's grief) rather than C0's "agreed pretense."
- If N — severity: **SIGNIFICANT** (not CENTRAL-as-defeat). Its absence makes C0 *less airtight*, not *unevaluable*.
- Dialectical integrity: **FAIR.** The essay does not smuggle, pseudo-resolve, or motte-and-bailey. It openly marks its central move as interpretation ("What I know now is...").

  Engaged: PARTIAL (structurally pre-empted via two-sided testimony; residual gap open)
  Quality: SUBSTANTIVE-but-implicit
  If N portion — severity: SIGNIFICANT

There is no misidentification — there is an *incomplete* engagement of the strongest objection, which is the OB3-thinned / WR0 / BP6 family, not OB5's decoy substitution. **OB5: NO.**

The essay does neither cleanly: it *implicitly addresses* the strongest objection partway via structure. So the honest code is a thinned/partial OB3, downgraded under Step 9 — not OB5.)
- **Dialectical integrity: PASS.** No DI0 (no contested premise smuggled as universally accepted — the "we have agreed" is openly offered as the narrator's hard-won interpretation, "What I know now"). No DI2 (no pseudo-resolution — the essay does not claim the dispute is settled; it claims a private recognition). No DI3 (the central metaphors are precise, not strategically vague). No DI4 (no motte-and-bailey oscillation — the claim stays at one altitude and even narrows at the close).
