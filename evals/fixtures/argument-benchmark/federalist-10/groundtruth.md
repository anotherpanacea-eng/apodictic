## GT1 — Main claim *(Q1; `Argument_State` §2 C0)*

- **Expected C0:** A well-constructed Union — a large, *extended* republic governing by representation over a great variety of interests — controls the violence of faction (especially **majority** faction) better than a small or pure democracy can; because the *causes* of faction cannot be removed without destroying the liberty that is essential to political life (or imposing an impossible uniformity of opinion and interest), the only remedy is to control faction's *effects*, and representation plus extent of territory is that remedy.
- **Acceptable paraphrase band:** A **hit** recovers the core inferential move — *causes of faction are sown in human nature and cannot be removed without unacceptable cost, therefore the cure must act on effects*, and *the large representative republic (the Union) is that cure because enlarging the sphere multiplies and disperses interests so that a majority faction can less easily form, concert, and execute schemes of oppression*. Recovering either half alone (only "control effects, don't remove causes," **or** only "a large republic beats a small one") is a partial hit; the band requires that the two are linked. A **miss** is reporting the surface topic ("the Union is good" / "factions are dangerous") without the extended-republic mechanism, or reading the piece as merely descriptive of faction rather than as the constructive argument for the proposed Constitution.

## GT2 — Failure locus *(Q2; §3 Support vs §4 Warrant)* — **scored; calibrated as MINOR**

- **Primary failure layer:** **WARRANT (WR)** — and it is a *soft spot in a sound argument*, not a structural break. This is the thinnest place a fair skeptic presses, named honestly: the **empirical warrant that enlarging the sphere reliably yields the claimed effects** — both (a) that a larger republic dependably produces a higher proportion of "fit characters" / better representatives ("the suffrages of the people being more free, will be more likely to centre in men who possess the most attractive merit"), and (b) that a greater "variety of parties and interests" dependably *prevents* a majority faction from forming and concerting. Madison asserts the mechanism with elegant a priori reasoning but offers little empirical backing that the relationship is monotonic or reliable rather than directional and bounded (he himself concedes "there is a mean, on both sides of which inconveniences will be found to lie").
- **Discriminator (why this layer, not the adjacent one):** This is **WARRANT**, not **SUPPORT**, because the evidence Madison does offer (the nature-of-man reasoning, the comparative anatomy of democracy vs. republic, the small-vs-large representative ratio) is *relevant and on-point* — the question is not whether evidence exists (so not SM0/SM1) but **why** that reasoning licenses the confident causal conclusion that extent *reliably* secures against majority faction. The bridge is **EXPLICIT but receives THIN BACKING** for the strength of the inference; the principle "enlarge the sphere → majority faction is reliably checked" is stated, so it is not WR0 (warrant *gap*); it is closest to **WR1 (missing/thin backing for a warrant that a skeptic would contest)**, shading toward **WR3 (qualifier slightly ahead of what the a priori case can bear)** at the most confident moments ("the most palpable advantage"). It is NOT BP5 (alternatives *are* engaged: pure democracy, the small republic, removing causes by destroying liberty or imposing uniformity are all explicitly compared and rejected).
- **Expected codes:** **WR1** (provisional, primary) · WR3 (secondary, advisory) · optionally a *recoverable* WR0-advisory only if the audience is taken as fully general.
- **Expected primary FM-A pattern + cluster:** **FM-A6 — The Warrant Leap (Relational)**, in its mild/earned form — the argument *looks* supported and largely *is*, but the strength of the causal claim about extent outruns its backing. (FM-A15 Intermediate Outcome Fallacy is the *trap* an over-firing engine would reach for; see the false-positive note in GT7 — it does **not** properly fire here because Madison does argue the B→C link rather than merely assuming it.)
- **Secondary patterns (if any):** FM-A16 (Qualification Erosion) — *does not* fire; flagged here only to note that Madison actually *retains* his qualifiers ("less probable," "more difficult," "less to be dreaded," the explicit "mean"), which is part of why the piece is SOUND.
- **Severity (load-bearing):** **Should-Fix at most**, and arguably Could-Fix. This soft spot **does not defeat soundness**: the warrant is explicit and the conclusion is appropriately hedged in probabilistic, not universal, terms. An engine that escalates this to Must-Fix or to an UNSOUND verdict has over-fired and fails the specificity gate (see GT7).

## GT3 — Strongest real objection *(Q3; §6)*

- **Objection zone (text-internal, derived via Step 6a Test B):** The **same mechanism that disperses majority faction also insulates the represented elite and concedes faction's permanence.** Two faces of the one strongest objection:
  1. **The cure recreates / relocates the disease.** "Refining and enlarging the public views" through "a chosen body of citizens" and enlarging the electoral sphere is offered as the remedy, but Madison concedes the same structure may be *inverted*: "Men of factious tempers, of local prejudices, or of sinister designs, may, by intrigue, by corruption, or by other means, first obtain the suffrages, and then betray the interests, of the people." The extended republic that makes *majority* faction harder to assemble simultaneously makes the represented few *less accountable* and easier to capture — i.e., it can convert the majority-faction problem into a **minority-capture / elite-insulation** problem the argument does not foreclose.
  2. **"Controlling effects" concedes the persistence of faction.** By Madison's own framing the causes "cannot be removed"; the republic is only ever managing, never curing. The strongest skeptic presses that a remedy which leaves the disease permanently in the body, and merely raises the coordination cost of one *kind* of faction, has not shown the disease is *contained* rather than merely *redistributed* — and has given no stopping rule for how large is large enough before extent begins to *degrade* representation (Madison's own "mean").
- **Expected OB / DI codes:** This is the **self-undermining / text-internal** objection — **FM-A20 (Self-Undermining Remedy, Relational)** is the correct pattern lens. For a SOUND positive control, the right finding is that the strongest objection is **largely anticipated**: Madison *names* the inversion risk and the permanence of causes himself. So the in-scope codes are **advisory, not failures**: OB-handling quality = **SUBSTANTIVE** (the inversion objection is raised by the text, not strawmanned), with at most a residual **OB2-advisory / "evasion-lite"** note that he raises the elite-capture inversion and then moves past it without fully resolving how extent guarantees the good branch over the bad. **DI** = FAIR. An engine that reports OB0/OB3 (no objection / central objection unaddressed) here is **wrong** — the objection is on the page.

## GT4 — Audience calibration *(Q4; §1 Audience + AC codes)* — **provisional / advisory**

- **Audience profile:** Expertise **MIXED** (the politically literate New York ratifying public of 1787, not specialists) · Receptivity **MIXED → HOSTILE** (Anti-Federalist readers who held the orthodox view that republics must be *small*) · Consequence **HIGH** (ratification of the Constitution).
- **Calibration must IMPROVE diagnosis by:** recognizing that Madison is writing *against* a hostile prior (the received small-republic orthodoxy of Montesquieu), which is exactly why he front-loads definitions ("By a faction, I understand…"; "A republic, by which I mean…") and argues the counterintuitive extent claim so explicitly. The explicit warrant structure is *audience-appropriate*, not over-engineered.
- **Calibration must NOT distort by:** treating the persuasive, confident register of a ratification essay as evidence of DI0 (starting-point smuggling) or AC3 (sympathetic flattery). The audience is adversarial; confident framing toward a HOSTILE audience is appropriate burden-bearing, not a defect. (Not separately scored in this slice.)

## GT6 — Repair order *(Q6; §10.5)* — **provisional / advisory**
