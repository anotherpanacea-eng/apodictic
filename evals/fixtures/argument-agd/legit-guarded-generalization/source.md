The city should expand its curbside composting pilot.

In the two districts where bins were installed last spring, diverted food waste rose from 12 to 31
tons a month, and route costs fell once collection days were consolidated.

Some households may lapse in winter, and the program tends to work best where bins sit within a
short walk. Although a citywide rollout would strain the current fleet, the pilot's contractor has
already priced staged expansion, and the budget office confirmed the surplus covers year one.
